= Scale system performance for growing traffic

[role="_abstract"]
When experiencing increased user demand and concurrent platform access, you need to scale system performance for growing traffic so that you can maintain responsive page load times, prevent service degradation, and ensure high availability across the developer hub.

== Why scale for performance

As your development organization grows and more teams adopt {product-short}, platform usage increases in both volume and concurrency. Without a scaling strategy, performance degradation becomes evident through:

* Slow page load times during peak usage hours
* Timeout errors when multiple teams access the catalog simultaneously
* Delayed plugin responses under high load
* Service interruptions during traffic spikes
* Degraded user experience that reduces platform adoption

Scaling {product-short} for performance ensures that response times remain consistent regardless of traffic volume, concurrent users maintain productive workflows, and the platform supports organizational growth without requiring frequent emergency interventions.

== How {product-short} enables performance scaling

{product-short} provides multiple scaling mechanisms that work together to handle growing traffic:

High availability architecture::
Distributes incoming requests across multiple backend replicas using built-in load balancing. When properly configured, replica scaling ensures that increased traffic spreads across available instances rather than overwhelming a single backend. This horizontal scaling approach maintains response times even during traffic spikes.

Dynamic plugins caching::
Reduces platform boot time and plugin initialization overhead by storing already-installed plugins. The cache eliminates redundant downloads and speeds up pod startup times during scaling events, ensuring new replicas become available quickly.

Plugin assets caching::
Accelerates asset loading by caching frequently accessed plugin resources. This reduces network traffic, decreases asset load times, and improves overall page rendering performance for users.

== Scaling workflow

Follow this workflow to implement performance scaling for growing traffic:

. *Plan your scaling strategy* by assessing your current traffic patterns, estimating future growth, and determining appropriate replica counts for your target scale level.

. *Configure high availability* to distribute load across multiple backend replicas and enable automatic failover during instance failures.

. *Enable dynamic plugins cache* to reduce platform startup times and ensure new replicas become available quickly during scaling operations.

. *Enable plugin assets cache* to improve asset loading performance and reduce the load on backend services.

Monitor performance metrics after implementation to validate that your scaling configuration meets your response time targets and adjust replica counts or resource allocations as traffic patterns evolve.
